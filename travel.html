Getting to

Newfoundland

Since Newfoundland is an island, we recommend traveling by air. The main and closest airport is the St. John's International Airport (YYT) which is a short 20 minute drive from the downtown area. If you prefer a more scenic trip, you can drive. It is about a 3 day trip from Ontario with a ferry crossing from Nova Scotia to the West Coast of Newfoundland.

PS. Accommodations and rental cars in St. John's can be difficult to find, especially during the summer months. We highly recommend that you reserve your stay and transportation as early as possible. The city typically books out months in advance during the summer season as it is a popular travel destination.

Getting Around

The best way to explore St. John's and the surrounding sights is by car. Rental cars are fully booked at this point however TURO recently came to Newfoundland and they may have a few options left. You can also try your luck with Enterprise Car Rentals as they might have a cancellation.

Taxis are available throughout the city and there is public transit, the metro bus, however it is quite infrequent. There is also a hop-on-hop-off bus that travels to popular attractions.

Where to Eat (& Drink)

Downtown St. John's is filled with local restaurants and cafes. You'll find fresh seafood, local craft beer, and the best fish & chips on earth. It is also home to the well-known George Street which houses over 23 lively bars within one block. After a day of exploring, stop by for a drink (or three) and grab some late night fries from the ziggys truck on your walk home.

Things to Do

As the most easterly point in North America, Newfoundland is known for its rugged coastline and natural beauty. Embrace the opportunity and hike a leg of the East Coast Trail or explore outside the city and book a picnic in Ferryland (you won't regret it). If you don't wish to rent a car, catch the hop-on-hop-off bus to historical lookouts like Signal Hill and Cape Spear, or jump off in the Quidi Vidi Gut and explore the tiny streets. If the weather is poor (which it often is) take some time to emmerse yourself in the Newfoundland history and culture by visiting the Rooms Museum, local shops, or the geo centre.
